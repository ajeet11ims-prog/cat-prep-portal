Set Information

Plenary session of International Economics Association was held at New York in October 2015. Total 56 researchers attended the session. Each researcher was an expert in one of the following areas in Economics: Microeconomics, Macroeconomics, International Economics and Econometrics. The researchers were from one of the following countries: USA, UK, Canada and Australia. The following information is also known.

  1. Had there been two experts less from UK, Canada and Australia each, the number of experts from USA would have been twice that of from each of the other three countries.
  2. The number of experts in Macroeconomics was equal to the number of experts in International Economics.
  3. The number of experts in Microeconomics was 8.
  4. There were at least 2 and at most 6 researchers in each of the four areas from each of the four countries.
  5. The number of experts in International Economics was equal to the number of experts in Econometrics.

Q1. It is known that the number of researchers in at least two areas out of the given four areas from the UK, Canada and Australia are equal. Further, the pair of two areas having equal number of researchers was unique for each of these three countries. Which of the following cannot be correct?

Q2. Consider the following two statements:

I: The number of researchers from the UK in exactly three areas were equal.

II: The number of researchers from Canada in Macroeconomics and Econometrics was equal.

What can be said about these two statements?
