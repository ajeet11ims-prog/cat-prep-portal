A laptop manufacturing company, ABC Limited, operates in a metropolitan city with five major distributors: X1, X2, X3, X4, and X5. The company is evaluating the sales performance of these distributors across different laptop models. ABC Limited offers two categories of laptops: Gaming and Business.

Each category consists of two models: High-end and Standard.

Gaming laptops accounted for 25% of the total laptops sold in the city, while the remaining were Business laptops. Among the High-end laptops, 75% were Business laptops.

The following information is known:

  1. Each distributor sold at least one High-end Business laptop. X1 sold 15 High-end laptops, while X3 sold 7 Standard laptops.
  2. Across the city, a total of 9 Standard Gaming laptops and 60 High-end Business laptops were sold. X5 sold a total of 8 Business laptops.
  3. The number of Business laptops sold by X1 was twice the number of Gaming laptops sold by the same distributor.
  4. X3 and X4 sold an equal number of Gaming laptops, which was one-fourth of the number sold by X2.
  5. X2 and X3 were the only distributors that sold Standard Gaming laptops, with X2 selling twice as many as X3.
  6. X3 and X4 sold the same number of High-end Business laptops, which was one-third of the number sold by X2.
  7. The number of High-end Gaming laptops sold by X5 was equal to the number of Standard Gaming laptops sold by X2.
  8. The number of Standard Business laptops sold by X2 is twice the total number of Gaming laptops sold by X5.
