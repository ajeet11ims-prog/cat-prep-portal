Question No. 25 Marks for correct answer: 3 | Negative marks: 1

Saurabh is a PhD student presenting his research to a panel of three professors at his university. The panelists provided him with a list of 15 questions to prepare for his presentation. Panelist 1 asked questions 1 to 5, panelist 2 asked questions 6 to 10, and panelist 3 asked questions 11 to 15. Saurabh was required to answer 3 out of the 5 questions posed by each panelist.

Each selected question had to be addressed from one of three perspectives: Theory, Application, or Innovation, ensuring that each of these aspects was covered exactly once per panelist. The maximum possible score for a question was 20, and all the panelists awarded marks that were prime numbers, with only the lowest score being repeated once.

It is further known that:

  1. Saurabh was awarded a maximum of 33 marks by panelist 3 and a minimum of 20 marks by panelist 1. He did not answer the first and last questions asked by panelist 3.
  2. The total marks obtained in Theory, Application, and Innovation were 26, 29, and 24, respectively.
  3. The marks obtained in two questions were exactly one-fourth of the respective question numbers, whereas for another question, the score was equal to the question number.
  4. The minimum and maximum marks were obtained in consecutive questions from different panelists for Innovation and Application respectively.
  5. The second-highest and second-lowest marks were not awarded for Theory.
  6. The marks for questions 1 and 2 were in decreasing order. Additionally, Saurabh did not answer any pair of consecutive questions from only panelist 2.
  7. After observing his performance, the panelists allowed Saurabh to reconsider and reattempt all the questions in which he had obtained less than 35% marks. As a result, he managed to increase his total score by 40 marks. All the revised scores remained prime numbers.
Q.25 [2450309]

What was his initial score in question number 10?

Q.26 [2450309]

Which of the following is the correct order of question numbers on the basis of the initial marks obtained by Saurabh?

Q.27 [2450309]

If, after the retest, the scores for all eligible questions definitely increased, then how many possible combinations of the new scores exist, regardless of order?

II. The total marks awarded to him by panelist 1 were 40.
